Safety

Build Without Worry

Eliminating Operator Exposure to Worksite Hazards

When all PPE is worn, all guidance adhered to, all tasks executed perfectly, and everything goes as expected, the worksite is an exceptionally safe environment. However, accidents can always happen.

Construction related occupations make up the third most dangerous occupations in the United States. Over the past decade, TBD people have lost life or limb in worksite-related accidents. This has led to countless lawsuits, settlements, and regulations, not to mention the human impact on families of those injured or killed.

It is our belief that the safest place for operators is not inside the cab, but behind a desk, far from the potential hazards of the worksite.

Below are some of the hazards present in nearly every worksite nationwide:

Airborne Particulate

Worksites are full of dust / asbestos / small particulate matter.

Noxious Gasses

Equipment onsite constantly emit harmful gasses.

Extreme Noise

Construction, destruction, and even regular site prep often require loads of loud equipment. OSHA's permissible exposure limit for an 8 hour workday is just 90dBA, about the level of a blender.

Accidental Strikes

Worksites are by definition constantly changing environments full of large and powerful equipment. Anything from weather conditions to equipment wear and tear can culminate in workplace accidents.

Unstable Soil

Inadequately prepared soil can result in unexpected movement of heavy equipment.

Environmental Hazards

Any number of hazards, from blocked lines of sight to poor ground visibility, can exacerbate already existing hazards on the worksite.
